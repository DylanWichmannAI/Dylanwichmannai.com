This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information when you visit our website, contact us, or engage our services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information You Provide

  • Contact details: Name, email address, phone number
  • Business information: Company name, industry, business size, location
  • Communication content: Messages, enquiries, and correspondence you send us
  • Service-specific information: Details about your workflows provided during consultations
  • Payment information: Billing details processed via secure third-party payment providers

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: Pages visited, time on site, click behaviour, referral source
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, device type, operating system
  • Cookies and tracking technologies: Session and analytics cookies (see Section 8)

2.3 Information From Third Parties

  • Publicly available business information when relevant to a service enquiry
  • Referral information when referred by an existing client or partner

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Service Delivery

  • Respond to enquiries and provide requested information
  • Deliver AI consulting, deployment, and coaching services
  • Process payments and issue invoices
  • Manage ongoing client relationships and support

3.2 Business Operations

  • Improve our services based on feedback and usage patterns
  • Maintain internal records and administration
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Detect and prevent fraud or security incidents

3.3 Marketing (with consent only)

  • Send newsletters or updates about our services — only with your explicit consent
  • You may withdraw consent at any time by emailing admin@dylanwichmannai.com
We will never: Sell your personal information to third parties. Use your data for undisclosed purposes. Process your data in ways that contradict this policy.

4. Data Protection & Security

4.1 Technical Measures

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted uses TLS/HTTPS encryption
  • Encryption at rest: Sensitive stored data is encrypted
  • Access controls: Personal data is accessible only to authorised personnel
  • Secure hosting: Industry-standard infrastructure and security practices

4.2 Organisational Measures

  • Strict internal data handling policies
  • Regular review of data practices and security controls
  • Minimal data collection — we only keep what we need

4.3 Data Breach Response

In the event of a security breach posing risk to your personal information, we will notify affected individuals and the Information Regulator within POPIA-required timeframes.

6. Your Rights Under POPIA

As a data subject under POPIA, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

👁️

Right to Access

You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.

✏️

Right to Correction

You may request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal information.

🗑️

Right to Deletion

You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to applicable legal retention requirements.

🚫

Right to Object

You may object to the processing of your personal information for direct marketing purposes at any time.

📦

Right to Portability

You may request your personal information in a structured, machine-readable format.

⚖️

Right to Complain

You may lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at admin@dylanwichmannai.com. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Third-Party Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties.

We may share limited data with trusted service providers under strict data processing agreements:

  • Hosting & infrastructure: Cloud providers for website and system hosting
  • Analytics: Website analytics (anonymised/aggregated data only)
  • Payment processing: Secure payment gateways under their own privacy policies
  • Communication tools: Email service providers for business correspondence

All third-party providers are contractually obligated to process data only on our instructions, maintain appropriate security, and delete data upon termination.

Legal Disclosures

We may disclose personal information if required by South African law, court order, or legitimate government authority request.
